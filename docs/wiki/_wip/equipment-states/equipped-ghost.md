# Equipped Ghost

Equipment fused to other equipment loses all normal physics behaviours. With overload tech, you can equip this fusion separately, making it one drop away from being a ghost.

## Method

1. [Overload drop](uid:2U2) the target item.
1. Fuse the overload dropped item to a weapon (for shields and bows) or shield (for weapons and bows).
1. Fail drop the item to make it an equipped ghost.

## Effects

- Equipped ghosts largely behave like normal equipment for as long as they remain equipped.
- Their size is influenced by the previous fuse parent, so they may appear larger than normal.
- Dropping the equipment will ghost it; it will float in mid-air, interacting with nothing and being unable to be picked up.
    - If the equipment is fused to a shield, you can move out of the ghost's update range and detangle the shield. Returning to the ghost will undo all ghost effects and return the equipment to normal.
- Warping with the parent equipped (but with the ghost dropped) will re-attach it to the parent like a normal fuse.
- Warping with the parent _and_ child equipped will immediately ghost the item at the warp destination. If you warp somewhere else, the ghost will move there. Fail dropping it won't reconnect it to you anymore.
- You can freely drop and re-equip the original fuse parent without affecting the ghost.
- If you drop (and ghost) the fused equipment while the parent is dropped, the ghost will still be selectable with Ultrahand and Recall. (Targeting either will only ever activate the ability on the parent.)
